How to Conserve Energy

Use Energy-Efficient Appliances

How: Upgrade to appliances with high Energy Star ratings, such as LED bulbs, refrigerators, air conditioners, and washing machines.

Impact: These appliances use less power, reducing overall energy consumption in homes and businesses

Implement Smart Thermostats

How: Install smart thermostats to automatically adjust heating and cooling based on occupancy and time of day.

Impact: Optimizing indoor temperatures can save significant amounts of energy without sacrificing comfort.

Install Solar Panels

How: Invest in rooftop solar panels to generate your own electricity from the sun, reducing reliance on grid power.

Do's And Dont's

Do's For Conservation

  • Choose appliances with energy star ratings, which consume less electricity for the same performance.
  • Switch off lights, fans, and electronics when not in use to save energy.
  • Replace traditional bulbs with energy-efficient LED lights to reduce electricity consumption.
  • Proper insulation helps maintain temperature, reducing the need for excessive heating or cooling.
  • Make the most of daylight by designing spaces that allow sunlight in, reducing the need for artificial lighting.
  • Devices left on standby still consume energy, so unplug chargers, TVs, and computers when not needed.
  • Regular maintenance of air conditioners and heaters improves their efficiency, reducing energy waste.
    • Know More

Don'ts For Conservation

  • Leaving appliances on standby or lights on when not in use leads to unnecessary energy consumption.
  • Avoid using outdated or inefficient devices that consume more energy than newer, energy-efficient models.
  • Poor insulation causes heat loss in winter and heat gain in summer, making heating and cooling systems work harder.
  • Setting thermostats too high in winter or too low in summer wastes energy. Optimize the temperature settings.
  • Ignoring maintenance for appliances like HVAC systems or refrigerators reduces their efficiency and increases energy use.
  • Not making use of natural light during the day leads to unnecessary lighting energy consumption.
  • Even when not in use, plugged-in devices like chargers can draw "phantom" energy.
