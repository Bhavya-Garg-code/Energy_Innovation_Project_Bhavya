Description: Microgrids are localized energy grids that can operate independently of the traditional grid. This project involves designing a small, smart microgrid using renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, or bioenergy to provide electricity to remote or underserved communities.

Innovation: By incorporating IoT and AI for energy management, this microgrid can optimize energy distribution and reduce waste, making electricity supply more reliable and sustainable.

Applications: Rural and off-grid communities, disaster relief, or areas with contaminated water sources.

Description: This project involves designing a small-scale prototype or model of a vehicle powered by hydrogen fuel cells. Hydrogen fuel cells produce electricity through a chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen, with water as the only byproduct.

Innovation: Hydrogen is a clean and abundant energy source, and fuel cells provide a zero-emission alternative to traditional internal combustion engines.

Applications: Transportation, especially for heavy-duty vehicles like trucks and buses, and backup power systems.

Tesla Powerwall and Powerpack are rechargeable lithium-ion batteries that store energy from solar or the grid. Powerwall is for residential use, while Powerpack is designed for commercial-scale energy storage and backup.
